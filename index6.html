Virat Kohli is a top-order Indian cricketer, born in Delhi on November 5, 1988, widely considered one of the greatest batsmen of his generation. He has captained India in all formats and is known for his aggressive batting style and numerous records, including being the fastest to 10,000 ODI runs. He married Indian actress Anushka Sharma in 2017 and they have two children. Early life and career Born: November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. Family: He grew up in a Punjabi family in Delhi; his father was a lawyer and his mother was a homemaker. Youth career: He led the Indian U-19 team to victory in the 2008 World Cup in Malaysia. International debut: He made his ODI debut in 2008 and his Test debut in 2011. Cricket achievements and records Captaincy: He captained India in all three formats (Test, ODI, T20I) and is India's most successful Test captain. Batting records: Holds the record for the most centuries in One Day Internationals (50). Is the fastest player to reach many milestones, including 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 ODI runs. Has scored the most runs in a single T20I innings and has the highest career batting average in T20Is. World Cup: He was part of the winning Indian team in the 2011 Cricket World Cup and the winning T20 World Cup in 2024. Awards: He has won numerous awards, including ICC Cricketer of the Year in 2017 and 2018, and the Padma Shri in 2017. Personal life Marriage: He married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in 2017. Family: They have two children, a daughter born in 2021 and a son born in 2024. Philanthropy: He is involved in philanthropy through his foundation, which supports young athletes.
